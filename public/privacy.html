We protect your privacy in a very simple way: We never ask for any private information.
Your public key is just that, public. You can share it with whomever you want without
concern.

However, the Public PGP Key Signer treats your public key as private information.
Your key is stored on our server only until you click on the confirmation link and
receive your signed key. Immediately afterwards the Public PGP Key Signer will delete
your public key from it's internal keyring. Once you have received your signed key
we have no direct way of knowing who you are or what your email address is.

For the more technically inclined
The Public PGP Key Signer does keep a log of the public key fingerprints which it has
signed, indirectly. This is because the confirmation link you are sent is your fingerprint
and some random characters signed by our private key. Since we log which URLs have
been hit we can retrieve these fingerprints. However, unless you have uploaded your
key somewhere else, e.g. to the MIT key server we cannot use the fingerprint to find your key.
